Central Cave

Central Cave — dubbed the “Crystal Paradise of Samar” — lies roughly 5–8 kilometers from Catbalogan proper, in Barangay Kawayan (or near San Andres depending on the source). To get inside, visitors must descend about 18 meters — it’s a vertical entry via rope, dropping you into a looped chamber system.

Inside, Central Cave isn’t huge compared to some mega‑caves out there, but it more than makes up for it with jaw‑dropping interior: expect crystalline, white‑crystal formations, dramatic stalactites, stalagmites, flowstones, and even cave pearls — basically a subterranean art gallery crafted by nature.The cave’s “loop + branch + lower level” layout adds a little exploration vibe without being overly technical.

Getting there isn’t exactly a stroll though — you first ride (usually via tricycle or jeep) from Catbalogan to the jump‑off point (often via a local water‑pumping station or community access road), then trek through forest/woodland and terrain for maybe 1–3 hours before reaching the entrance. Because of that, exploring Central Cave tends to be for the adventurous: rappelling, hiking, navigating slippery rocks, maybe some river‑crossings depending on the route — but the reward? A magical underground world.
