Perseveranda Catbalogan

Pieta Park was created as a memorial to honor the victims of MV Doña Paz tragedy (1987) — considered the deadliest peacetime maritime disaster in history.Its main feature was a marble replica of La Pietà, which many locals and families of the victims treated as a symbol of remembrance and mourning. The park sits right across from St. Bartholomew Church — making it a central, visible part of the city’s public/historic core.

Unfortunately — and this is heavy — the park was recently demolished in 2025. The city government says demolition work started without proper permits. Many residents were shocked and outraged because the memorial — especially the La Pietà statue — held deep emotional and cultural significance for Catbalogan. The statute was reportedly damaged in the process (the head of Mary was removed, among other destruction).

The justification given by the church authorities — who own the land — was that the park had deteriorated over the years and become neglected, even described as a dumpsite or unmaintained space before the demolition initiative. Still — a lot of locals view this act as a loss of heritage, a painful erasure of collective memory tied to a tragic event that deeply affected the community.
