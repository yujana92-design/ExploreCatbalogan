Buri Baras Beach

Buri Baras Beach sits on Buri Island, roughly 10–12 km from Catbalogan’s city proper — reachable via an embankment/bridge that links the island to the mainland. It offers a long coastal stretch that’s ideal for swimming and diving, making it one of the most loved coastal escapes for both locals and tourists.

What’s nice about Buri Baras is its chill, easy‑going vibe: water quality is generally good, and there are affordable cottages for overnight stays or day‑use — a solid choice whether you’re bonding with family, friends, or just need a solo nature fix. Even when the sand isn’t the classic “white‑beach” type, the beach still draws people because of the calm waters and peaceful setting.

Beyond just “swim‑and‑tan,” Buri Baras serves as part of a broader tourism push for Buri Island: local initiatives aim to balance tourism with ecological awareness — there have been efforts to develop the island sustainably, combining community livelihood, mangrove‑conservation and tourism amenities. It’s not just a beach; it’s like a little gateway to chill, nature, and a slower island‑life vibe — good for resetting and low‑key adventures.
