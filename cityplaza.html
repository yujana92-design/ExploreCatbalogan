Catbalogan City Plaza

Catbalogan City Plaza is the main public plaza in downtown Catbalogan City. It features a distinctive monument to José Rizal — his bust stands atop his novels ( Noli Me Tangere and El Filibusterismo ), held by three stylized figures. That unusual design (by a local sculptor) makes the plaza stand out as both civic space and a nod to history & identity.

This plaza isn’t just for picture‑taking. It’s a hangout spot: open spaces, a stage, and even facilities that support community events, sports (some say basketball, skate‑practice), and city celebrations. Local festivals sometimes use the plaza as venue.

A few years back, the plaza got a refresh — new water fountains, lighting, vertical gardens, and greeneries were added to revamp the look and make it more welcoming and modern. Now it’s more than a historic landmark — it’s a chill urban space: good for jogging, relaxing, or just vibing with friends/fam.
